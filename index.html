Property

92 keys. Palm Canyon Drive. Irreplaceable.

411 E. Palm Canyon Drive. Opened in 1964 by hotelier Ken Kimes as the most elaborate of his five Polynesian themed hotels. The Tropics was the south end of Palm Springs' answer to the Racquet Club: a regular destination for the Rat Pack, Elvis Presley, and Nancy Sinatra. Victor Mature had his own table in the Congo Room steakhouse. The underground Cellar bar seated 100. In 2001, the property became the birthplace of Tiki Oasis, now the largest tiki event in the world. Tiki Caliente, a dedicated annual festival, still draws a loyal audience to the property every year.

The Pool Is the Product

100,000 gallon pool, the third largest at any hotel in Palm Springs. Tiki bar. Cabanas. The pool is the social engine of the property: pool parties, DJ sets, weekend brunches, and curated events that generate ancillary revenue and organic reach. In a desert market where summer heat softens demand, this property becomes a destination precisely when others go quiet.

Optimized Scale

92 keys balances boutique intimacy with operational efficiency. Large enough to sustain energy, programming, and food and beverage revenue. Small enough to maintain high occupancy and per key economics across both peak and shoulder seasons without the overhead exposure of a 200+ key box.

What The Tropics Becomes

An immersive Polynesian boutique resort built around a 100,000 gallon pool and full scale outdoor social campus. Redesigned guestrooms with private patios. An expanded tiki bar and cabana deck. Polynesian influenced food and beverage with spectacle driven cocktails, open fire BBQ, and high margin beverage programming. Weekend social programming, poolside wellness, and curated events that make the property a social destination, not just a place to sleep. High design, accessible pricing, a hospitality culture that matches the concept, and a cultural identity that no competitor can replicate.

Market

Palm Springs competitive landscape

One of the highest RevPAR leisure markets in the western U.S. for eight months of the year. The Tropics enters a supply constrained boutique segment at a basis that no existing competitor can match.

01

Festival Compression

Coachella and Stagecoach drive annual rate surges across the Valley. L’Horizon commands ~$4,000 per night at peak. Year round events, midcentury design tourism, and a growing remote work leisure segment sustain demand well beyond festival weeks.

02

No New Boutique Supply

Zero boutique hotel projects in the pipeline. Tightening Short-Term Rental (STR) regulations continue to push demand back into licensed hotels. The segment is structurally undersupplied, and The Tropics enters at a fraction of replacement cost.

03

Summer Outperformance

Select properties outperform through summer via programming and positioning. The Saguaro demonstrates this at scale: pool centric, lifestyle driven, filling rooms when others contract. The Tropics targets the same lane with a more distinctive identity, stronger per key economics, and a more accessible price point than ultra luxury competitors.

Property Heritage & Cultural Substantiation
Opening & Developer
Built in 1963, opened in 1964 by Ken Kimes Sr. as "The Tropics." Number 38 of 45 motels Kimes developed. The most elaborate of five Polynesian themed properties in his chain (sister locations in Indio, Modesto, Blythe, and Rosemead).
Source: Caliente Tropics Resort official history; Tiki Oasis official event description; mytiki.life
Oceanic Arts Provenance
Tiki gods and artifacts throughout the resort were designed and manufactured by Oceanic Arts (Whittier, CA), founded 1956 by LeRoy Schmaltz and Bob Van Oosting. Oceanic Arts also created decor for Disney’s Enchanted Tiki Room, Trader Sam’s at Disneyland, Walt Disney World’s Polynesian Village Resort, Trader Vic’s, and Don the Beachcomber. Artist Ed Crissman carved 24 tikis from palm trunks onsite at The Tropics. Oceanic Arts closed permanently on November 24, 2021 after 65 years.
Source: Caliente Tropics official history; Coachella Valley Independent; avoidingregret.com; disneyhistory101.com; mytiki.life
Celebrity & Cultural History
Frequented by members of the Rat Pack and professional baseball teams. Elvis Presley and Nancy Sinatra were regulars at the pool. Victor Mature had a reserved table in the Congo Room steakhouse. The underground Cellar bar seated 100 guests. Described as the south end of Palm Springs’ equivalent to the Racquet Club.
Source: Caliente Tropics official history; Yelp; iamnotastalker.com; travelbyvacationrental.com
Birthplace of Tiki Oasis
Tiki Oasis, now the largest tiki event in the world, was founded at The Tropics on August 18, 2001 as a fundraiser to support the rehabilitation of the property. The event ran at the resort from 2001 to 2005 before outgrowing the venue and relocating to San Diego. The property continues to host Tiki Caliente and other annual tiki events.
Source: Times of San Diego; The Atomic Grog; reverberationsmedia.com; avoidingregret.com; mytiki.life
Pool
100,000 gallon pool, cited as the third largest at any hotel in the Coachella Valley. The pool retains its original 1964 configuration.
Source: iamnotastalker.com; Kayak hotel listing
Film Appearances
Featured in Alpha Dog (2006; dir. Nick Cassavetes; starring Justin Timberlake, Anton Yelchin, Amanda Seyfried) and City of Industry (1997; starring Harvey Keitel, Timothy Hutton).
Source: Visit Palm Springs official blog; iamnotastalker.com; Palm Springs Life
