The Opportunity

Rat Pack roots. Largest pool in Palm Springs.

Originally opened in 1964 as "The Tropics" by hotelier Ken Kimes, this property was a regular haunt of the Rat Pack during Palm Springs' golden era. It features the largest swimming pool of any hotel in the city — a 100,000-gallon signature amenity — and retains its original Polynesian A-frame architecture, tiki carvings, and courtyard layout. In a market dominated by cookie-cutter boutique conversions, The Tropics is irreplaceable.

01

One of a Kind

Authentic 1964 Polynesian architecture — original A-frame entrance, hand-carved tikis, courtyard layout. There is no comparable asset in the Coachella Valley.

02

100,000-Gallon Pool

The largest hotel swimming pool in Palm Springs. A proven draw for leisure travelers, event bookings, and social media visibility year-round.

03

Cultural Cachet

Rat Pack history. Annual Tiki Caliente festival. Featured in mid-century design tours. A built-in audience of enthusiasts and repeat visitors that money cannot buy.

Timing

Why now

Discounted basis, a local operating edge, macro tailwinds, and a phased entry structure create an unusually asymmetric entry point.

01

Below-Replacement Basis

The Tropics is available at $7.75M — approximately $84K per key. The Ace Hotel & Swim Club, two doors down, traded at ~$335K per key in 2022. New-build cost in Palm Springs exceeds $350K per key. Even after a $5.5M renovation, the all-in basis of ~$157K per key creates meaningful built-in downside protection.

02

Local Operating Edge

LBHG operates two hotels within 15 minutes of this property. The Johnston family has a 25-year track record in the Coachella Valley with established vendor relationships, staffing pipelines, and market knowledge that an outside buyer cannot replicate.

03

Palm Springs Tailwinds

Coachella / Stagecoach drive annual compression. L’Horizon rates now reach ~$4,000 per night. Short-term rental regulations continue to tighten, pushing demand back into licensed hotels. The boutique segment is supply-constrained with no new inventory pipeline.

04

Phased Entry, Defined Exit

Building-lease takeover September 2026 provides operational control before the SBA purchase in March 2027. The $5.5M renovation phases through 2027–2028, with an independent rebrand targeted for January 2028. Year 5 refinance returns partner capital; Year 5 exit is the base case.

Structure

Transaction structure & partner economics

Building-lease takeover in September 2026, SBA-financed purchase in March 2027, $5.5M phased renovation, and an independent boutique rebrand by January 2028. Partner capital ($2.0M loan + $627K equity) earns 8% cumulative interest on the loan beginning in Year 2 and equity distributions beginning in Year 4. The $2.0M partner loan is repaid first at the Year 5 refinance.
