The Opportunity

Discounted entry. Proven operator. Experiential edge.

Acquire a one of a kind Polynesian resort at $84K per key — less than a quarter of recent comparable trades — with a 30-year local operating team, a $5.5M repositioning plan, and a pool driven lifestyle concept built to outperform when competitors go dark in summer.

01

Why This Asset

Opened in 1964, The Tropics retains its original A frame entrance, hand carved tikis, and an iconic 100,000-gallon pool — the largest of any hotel in Palm Springs. Irreplaceable architecture in a market of cookie cutter conversions.

02

Why This Team

Four principals with 100+ combined years of hospitality ownership, operations, and investment — from Yellowstone to Relais & Châteaux to $1.5B in institutional transactions. Two properties within 15 minutes. Local vendor relationships, staffing pipelines, and market knowledge that outside capital cannot replicate.

03

Why This Concept

Palm Springs is a top RevPAR leisure market eight months a year. Most hotels struggle in summer heat. The Tropics’ water centric, tiki bar driven programming is designed to turn on demand when competitors turn off — capturing the months others leave on the table.

Property

92 keys. Palm Canyon Drive. Irreplaceable.

411 E. Palm Canyon Drive — the primary commercial corridor in Palm Springs. Originally opened in 1964 by hotelier Ken Kimes, The Tropics was a regular haunt of the Rat Pack and remains a fixture of the city’s mid century design culture. Tiki Caliente, the annual festival, draws a built in audience of enthusiasts and repeat visitors.

The Right Size

92 keys is the ideal balance: large enough to sustain energy, programming, and food & beverage revenue, but small enough to maintain high occupancy and operational efficiency — even in summer. Not too small to feel dead. Not too large to feel empty.

Water Is the Concept

100,000-gallon pool. Tiki bar. Cabana culture. The entire guest experience is organized around water and outdoor social space. In a desert market where summer heat empties most hotels, this property becomes a destination — pool parties, events, and programming that turns seasonal weakness into strength.

Timing

Why now

Palm Springs is one of the strongest RevPAR leisure markets in the western U.S. for roughly eight months a year. Below replacement pricing, tightening short term rental regulation, and a supply constrained boutique segment create an asymmetric entry point.

01

Below Replacement Basis

The Tropics is available at $7.75M — approximately $84K per key. The Ace Hotel & Swim Club, two doors down, traded at ~$335K per key in 2022. New build cost in Palm Springs exceeds $350K per key. Even after a $5.5M renovation, the all in basis of ~$157K per key creates meaningful built in downside protection.

02

Local Operating Edge

LBHG operates two hotels within 15 minutes of this property. The Johnston family has a 30-year track record in the Coachella Valley — owner and operator, not just a management company. Established vendor relationships, staffing pipelines, and market knowledge that an outside buyer cannot replicate.

03

Palm Springs Tailwinds

Coachella / Stagecoach drive annual compression. L’Horizon rates now reach ~$4,000 per night. Short term rental regulations continue to tighten, pushing demand back into licensed hotels. The boutique segment is supply constrained with no new inventory pipeline.

04

Phased Entry

Building lease takeover September 2026 provides operational control before the SBA purchase in March 2027. The $5.5M renovation phases through 2027–2028, with the independent rebrand targeted for January 2028. Disciplined, staged capital deployment — not a single large check into an unproven operation.

Market

Palm Springs market positioning

One of the highest RevPAR leisure markets in the western U.S. for eight months of the year — driven by Coachella Valley tourism, festival compression, tightening STR regulation, and zero new boutique supply in the pipeline. The Tropics is positioned to capture both the peak season premium and the off season opportunity that most competitors abandon.

01

Demand Drivers

Coachella and Stagecoach festivals drive annual rate compression. L’Horizon now commands ~$4,000/night at peak. Year round events, mid century design tourism, and a growing remote work leisure segment underpin demand well beyond festival season.

02

Supply Constraints

No new boutique hotel inventory in the pipeline. Short term rental regulations continue to tighten, pushing demand back into licensed hotels. The boutique segment is structurally undersupplied — and The Tropics enters at a fraction of replacement cost.

03

The Summer Edge

Most Palm Springs hotels see occupancy collapse in summer. The Tropics is built for heat: 100,000-gallon pool, tiki bar, cabana culture, and water centric programming that turns the off season into a destination. While competitors contract, this property activates.
