Investment Thesis

Why now

Discounted basis, a local operating edge, macro tailwinds, and a phased entry structure create an unusually asymmetric entry point.

01

Exceptional Basis

The Ace Hotel & Swim Club sold for $60M in October 2022 — $335K per key. The Tropics is available at $7.75M, creating meaningful built-in downside protection and substantial upside on repositioning.

02

Proven Operator, Local Edge

LBHG brings 50+ years of operating experience and 35+ years in Palm Springs, anchored by the 90-room BW Plus Las Brisas. Experienced, market-specific execution from day one — not an abstract turnaround.

03

Desert Macro Tailwinds

Palm Springs draws 14.4M annual visitors and $7.4B of spending, while new supply remains constrained by zoning. That supports ADR growth and asset value for differentiated repositionings.

04

Phased Entry, Defined Exit

Building-lease takeover September 2026 provides control before the SBA purchase in March 2027. $5.5M renovation phases through 2027–2028, with a Design Hotels rebrand in January 2028. Partner capital returns at the Year 5 refinance, with $400K+ annual distributions through exit.

Critically, at 92 keys versus the Ace's 179, The Tropics has a meaningful inventory advantage: fewer rooms means less pressure to discount in shoulder and off-peak seasons to fill the house. If the Marriott Design Hotels conversion materializes, the rate ceiling moves higher still. Las Brisas already achieves $209 ADR in the select-service segment — a repositioned lifestyle resort should comfortably exceed that.

Addendum D

Why this structure wins

The economics favor patience

Building-lease entry September 2026 gives LBHG immediate operational control and time to begin the $650K Phase 1 renovation before the SBA purchase closes March 2027. The $7.75M purchase at $84K/key compares to the Ace Hotel’s $335K/key. Annual debt service of $929K is supported by NOI reaching $875K in Year 3 and $1,698K by Year 5.

We don't own during the transition

The building-lease period (September 2026 – March 2027) limits exposure before ownership is assumed. Phase 1 renovation begins during this window. After the SBA purchase, the $5.5M renovation program continues through 2027–2028, culminating in the Design Hotels rebrand in January 2028.

The SBA conversation is different in Year 3

By Year 5, the SBA/refi lender evaluates a recorded 99-year lease, 12+ months of stabilized NOI at $1,698K, a completed renovation and Design Hotels rebrand, and a clean operating track record with 70% occupancy and $230 ADR. That produces a property value of ~$20M and a refinance that returns the partner’s $2.0M loan plus equity.

When accelerated purchase becomes the play

The closing date is subject to BIA approval of the 99-year lease. If approval slips, the building-lease period extends, maintaining LBHG’s operational control and renovation progress. The phased structure adapts to regulatory timing rather than betting against it.
